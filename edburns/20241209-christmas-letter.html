Merry Christmas 2024

It has been eight years since my last Christmas letter and many things have changed in the big wide world and in my own life and family.

Blessings

I continue to be blessed in so many ways but as I near the halfway point of my 52nd year, I want to call out that my mom, who is in her 84th year, is doing well. She still lives in the house I grew up in, and my brother Brendan lives nearby. Mom still drives and has a more active social calendar than I ever will! I am so blessed they are both doing so well.

Academics

When last I wrote, Logan, who turns 19 this Christmas, was the big man on campus at Bear Lake Elementary. He is starting another journey toward being BMOC again as a first year student in the honors program in the Electrical Engineering program at University of Florida in Gainesville. He graduated as salutatorian from his class at Crooms Academy of IT. As in my last update, he still excels at math and has added so many other aspects of excellence I can’t list them all. He is active in campus life as a contributor to the Solar Gators electric vehicle team and a member of the IEEE student chapter. He is adjusting well to campus life and loves his apartment near campus. The diligence imparted by his mother is paying dividends, I continue to be impressed by his self-motivated work ethic.

Owen is now a senior at University of Central Florida and has achieved several unprecedented milestones. When I last wrote, Owen was just about to start high school. Since then, he also graduated as salutatorian from his class at Crooms Academy of IT. He has done research programs at University of Central Florida, University of Tennessee, Knoxville and Carnegie-Mellon University in Pittsburgh. He is the lead author of a paper on collaborative autonomous vehicles published in the Proceedings of the IEEE Vehicular Technology Conference. As if that was not enough, he co-founded, and subsequently sold, a Artificial Intelligence company to a local fintech. As with his brother, the diligence imparted by his mother is paying dividends, I continue to be impressed by his self-motivated work ethic.

Career

As for me, I am very fortunate that my 22 year involvement with Java technology opened the door to a position at Microsoft in June of 2019. I continue to work with Java on Microsoft Azure and manage an excellent team in Shanghai, China. This position affords me the opportunity to visit the team regularly and I’ve had the pleasure four times since starting. I hope to continue providing value to Microsoft shareholders as long as I can.

Otherwise

Amy and I have been amicably working through a divorce for four years, and remain good friends. We are united on supporting our two amazing sons. I am happy to be in a relationship with Kristin Buxton, who I first met as an undergraduate at UIUC way back in 1994. Kristin lives in Eugene,Oregon and I visit frequently.

Closing

My family and I wish you all the blessings of joy and happiness in 2025!
